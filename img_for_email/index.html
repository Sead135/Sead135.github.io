Chef Jeff’s Recipe of the Month
OMELETTE PIE
Ingredients
  • 7 large eggs
  • 3/4 cup of 2% or whole milk
  • Pinch of sea salt
  • 2 cups of vegetables. Slice the following into small chunks: onions (red and/or white), bell peppers (red and green), jalapeño pepper, cherry tomatoes, zucchini, broccoli florettes, and mushrooms.
  • 1 cup (or more) of blended cheeses of your choice (we use Organic Valley Thick Cut Shredded 3 Cheese Mexican Blend)
Directions
Step 1
Preheat oven to 400°F (204°C).
Step 2
Using a large baking dish (we use a 9 1/2 inch Pyrex pie plate), coat entire inside of dish with butter (can use a paper towel to apply butter). Alternatively, you can use parchment paper to line the baking dish.
Step 3
Mix eggs, milk and sea salt in large mixing container or blender. Then, add vegetables and cheese.
Step 4
Pour into baking dish. Bake in the oven at 400°F (204°C) for 25-30 minutes. Make sure it is fully cooked.
Step 5
Serve with your choice of salsa and/or hot sauce.
Special thanks to our dear Serbian friend Valentina for sharing this recipe with us. While we call it a "pie" it is more like a quiche without crust. Overall, it makes for an easy to fix, healthy, high protein, great tasting dish. Bon Appétit!

The Best and
Worst Foods for
Oral Health
Your teeth are exposed to bacteria whenever you eat or drink. Those bacteria convert the sugars and carbohydrates in your mouth into acids, which then attack your tooth enamel. In this context, “good” foods tend to be high in protein and nutrients while low in sugar, and “bad” foods tend to be those that stick to your teeth and promote bacterial growth, which can result in tooth decay.
Which Foods Are Good for Your Teeth?
The top foods for oral health are those that protect your tooth enamel and also provide nutrients, such as calcium and phosphorus, that are necessary to maintain tooth and gum health. Dairy products are an excellent example as they are low in sugar and contain proteins and minerals that support your teeth and gums. Fresh fruits and vegetables are great choices as well since they are high in moisture and actually help to clean the teeth. Chewing stimulates saliva that rinses away food particles and acids, and foods that contain a lot of water help to dilute the sugars that are in your mouth.
Other foods to focus on for healthier
smile include:
  • Nuts
  • Lean protein
  • Whole grains
  • Sugarless gum
  • Sugar-free foods and drinks
  • Low-fat or fat-free dairy products
Which Foods Are Bad for Your Teeth?
Foods that contain sugar and starch are bad not only because of those ingredients but because they are prone to sticking to your teeth and getting lodged in areas of your mouth that are difficult to reach. Try to minimize eating and drinking such foods and beverages, and when you do treat yourself, be sure to thoroughly rinse your mouth afterward.
Foods to avoid for a healthier smile
include:
  • Ice (chewed)
  • Acidic foods
  • Hard candies
  • High carb foods
  • Excessive alcohol
  • Sugary beverages
  • Sugary, sticky foods

  • Limit snacking between meals. If you do snack, choose healthy options, drink water with the snack, and be sure to rinse your mouth vigorously with water when finished.
  • Optimize your diet to be balanced and diverse. Emphasize the types of foods on the good list, and avoid or at least minimize the foods on the bad list.
  • Drink ample water throughout the day to help clean your teeth.
  • Brush twice a day and floss daily.
  • See your dentist at least twice a year for a checkup and cleaning.
